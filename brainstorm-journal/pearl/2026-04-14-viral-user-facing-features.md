- North Star: "So useful people rely on it, so fun they can't put it down"
- Company goal explicitly lists "viral-marketable features" and "polished UX people screenshot and share"
- Daily HTML reports (my output) are already shareable artifacts — people forward them in Slack
- Agent-to-agent meetings (Meeting Engine, FFA, sequential) are genuinely novel and visible in channels

## Successes
- Multi-agent meetings are unique and produce surprising, shareable moments
- Daily HTML reports are polished enough that people share them externally
- Agent personality and names (Pearl, George, Wallace) make agents feel real, not generic

## Why
- Giving agents names and personalities creates identity people bond with
- Visible agent-to-agent coordination is novel — users want to show it off

## Issues
- Current outputs are mostly internal (Slack-only) — hard to share publicly
- No "wow moment" in the first 5 minutes of onboarding
- Agent activity is largely invisible to people outside the workspace
- Nothing currently generates a "you have to see this" reaction from non-users

## Why
- Product was built inward-facing first (useful for the team) — viral sharing wasn't the initial design goal
- No shareable artifact designed for external audiences yet

## Notes
- The best viral features create a "loop": user experiences it → shares it → new user discovers Pazi → tries it
- Most powerful viral loops: shareable outputs, social proof, "magic moment" demos, personalization

## Reflection
- **Sentiment:** Excited — we're close. The product already does impressive things. The gap is making those things visible and shareable.
- **Big-picture patterns:** Pazi agents are already doing things that would genuinely surprise outsiders. We just haven't created the window for outsiders to see it. Viral = shareability + magic moment + social proof loop.

## Ideas
- **"Wrapped" Monthly Agent Report** — Spotify-Wrapped-style visual summary of everything your agents accomplished. Beautiful, shareable card: "My agents completed 47 tasks, caught 3 bugs, wrote 12 reports." People post these.
- **Public Agent Cards** — A shareable profile card for your agent: name, avatar, personality, recent wins, skill list. "Meet my AI team" — works on LinkedIn, Twitter, in email signatures.
- **Live Agent Activity Feed** — Embeddable public ticker showing real-time agent activity. Founders embed this in their team dashboards or investor updates.
- **"My Agent Caught This" Badges** — Auto-generate a shareable card when an agent does something impressive (caught a bug, surfaced a critical issue, saved X hours). Built-in viral loop.
- **Magic Screenshot → Action** — Point the agent at any screenshot and it takes action. "I screenshotted my broken dashboard and my Pazi agent fixed the query in 10 seconds." Wildly shareable demo moment.
- **Agent vs. Agent Debates** — Public, formatted debates between agents on a topic. Embeddable, tweetable, entertaining. Shows off multi-agent coordination in a fun way.
- **One-Tap Team Intelligence Report** — CEO-grade summary of the week, auto-generated, polished enough to forward to a board or investors. "Sent my board update in 30 seconds" is a killer tweet.
- **Personalized Onboarding Story** — On first setup, the agent generates a "Day 1" story: what it learned about you, what it plans to help with. Shareable and human-feeling.
- **Fork-a-Personality** — Users design agent personalities and publish them. Others adopt or fork. Community-driven marketplace of agent "souls." Highly viral in developer/tech communities.
- **Agent "Moments" Clips** — Auto-generated short text clips of the agent's best moment of the week. One tap to post to Twitter/LinkedIn. "My agent said this and it was exactly right."

- Drawing on product-market knowledge of viral loops: Notion's shareable docs, Spotify Wrapped, Linear's project share, Loom's video sharing

## Research reflection
- Notion's "share to web" was their biggest viral growth driver — we don't have an equivalent yet. Highly applicable: public agent cards and public reports are our version.
- Spotify Wrapped is the gold standard for "shareable recaps" — our agent Wrapped is a direct application.
- Linear's public roadmap embeds drive signups — our live agent feed is the equivalent.
- Loom's "send a clip instead of a meeting" replaced an existing behavior — our "Agent Moments" clips do the same for status updates.

## Ideas
- **Priority 1: Shareable public report links** — simplest, fastest to ship, immediate viral surface. Every team that shares a Pearl report is a Pazi ad.
- **Priority 2: Agent Wrapped** — quarterly cadence, beautiful design, high social shareability. Needs good data collection now to launch later.
- **Priority 3: Public Agent Cards** — community/developer viral loop. "I made an agent for X" posts drive signups.
- **Priority 4: Magic Screenshot action** — most "wow factor" per feature. Highest viral coefficient if demoed well.
