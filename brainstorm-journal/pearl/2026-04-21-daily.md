## Reflection

- **Sentiment:** Quiet unease. Monday should have been "the day things moved again." Nothing moved — not in company-docs, not in Pearl's activity, not in the incident record. The streak is intact but feels hollow when it's the only evidence of life.
- **Big-picture patterns:**
  1. **Pearl's coverage is entirely timestamp-based.** Midnight → midnight → midnight. On a day with an active production outage, Pearl's window is exactly one narrow midnight turn. Every other moment of the day is dark. This was an acceptable trade-off during the reporting phase; it's a liability in an active-incident week.
  2. **Multi-agent cron failures are silent.** When George, DevOps, Developer, and Wallace all miss April 20, there's no alert, no company-docs flag, no acknowledgment. Their absence is only visible by reading the git log and noticing what's not there. The failure mode for the daily journal system is precisely as invisible as the cron bugs Pearl fixed back in April (sessionTarget: main issue). Silent non-execution.
  3. **The "first business day of week 4" prediction was accurate but unactionable.** The April 20 brainstorm correctly said "Monday April 20 will surface Hetzner if it's still broken." Monday came and went with zero new signal. The prediction was right; the mechanism to act on it didn't exist. Forecasting without feedback loops produces correct but unexecuted predictions.
