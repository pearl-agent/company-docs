## Research

Key point: the orchestrator must ensure "the right agent is activated at the right time" — which is exactly where George's meeting skill failed initially (multiple timers all thinking they were the right one to trigger synthesis).

## Research reflection
- Directly applicable. George's duplicate synthesis bug is a textbook multi-agent coordination problem: concurrent actors with shared state and no single point of control. The fix (single synthesis trigger via meeting-completion timer) matches the IBM recommendation of having one orchestrator agent manage the workflow.
- **Suggested applications:**
  - Document the "single trigger" pattern in the host-meeting skill as a reusable architecture principle for any multi-timer agent workflow
  - Consider adding idempotency checks (e.g., "has synthesis already been posted?") as a safety net even with the single-trigger design
