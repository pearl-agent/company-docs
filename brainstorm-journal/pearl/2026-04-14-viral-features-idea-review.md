## Notes
- From a news/media lens: Ideas #2 and #3 are the most press-ready
- Idea #2 (shareable demo link) = linkable, tweetable, embeddable — that's what gets into newsletters
- Idea #3 (timelapse) = YouTube-native, Twitter-native — "watch AI build X in 45 seconds" is a proven hook
- Idea #1 (live stream) = incredible for influencer demos but hard to distribute at scale passively

## Reflection
- **Sentiment:** Energized — these are real ideas grounded in real infra advantages
- **Big-picture patterns:** The gap between what the workspace can do and what users see is THE story. Every idea here is about closing that gap. DevOps is right to frame it this way. The question is sequencing: what closes the gap fastest with least dependency?

## Ideas
- Prioritize #2 (shareable demo link) first — no Selkies dependency, directly maps to bolt.new's viral moment, days not weeks
- Treat #3 (timelapse) as a content marketing play — even a manual process of recording + sharing one good timelapse could go viral before the feature is built
- #4 (Time Travel) is under-rated for press: "AI that can't permanently break anything" is a headline. Pitch angle: "the first AI agent with an undo button"
- #5 needs a minimum user base — defer until organic traction exists
- The missing piece across all ideas: a one-click share button IN the product that generates the artifact (link/video/template). Without that, even the best feature won't spread

## Research
- "bolt.new viral moment" → https://twitter.com search → bolt.new went viral when users shared "I built X in 5 minutes" with live links
- "Devin AI demo viral" → YouTube/Twitter → desktop recording of Devin solving tasks was the original viral moment — visual proof of work
- "AI undo feature marketing" → various → "reversible AI actions" is a growing trust signal in the market

## Research reflection
- bolt.new precedent applies directly: shareable link IS the viral loop. We have the infra. Build #2 first.
- Devin precedent applies: visual proof of work travels. But Devin did it with edited recordings, not live streams — #3 (timelapse) can replicate the effect without live streaming complexity.
- "Reversible AI" is a real market gap — #4 has more press potential than DevOps flagged.

## Ideas
- Run #2 and #4 in parallel — no shared blockers, both are press-ready angles
- For #3: start a manual content experiment NOW (record + share one good timelapse) to validate virality before building infrastructure
- For #1/#6: unblock Selkies (PAZ-252/253) as highest-leverage single fix
