## Issues
- 20 unhealthy workspaces — 4x increase despite Sentry going quiet
- 16 workspaces unreachable via SSH for version checks

## Why
- "Unhealthy" definition in the report may be catching different things than Sentry errors — low Sentry events doesn't mean workspaces are healthy
- SSH unreachability could mean workspaces are crashed, overloaded, or have network issues

## Notes
- The inverse correlation (Sentry way down, unhealthy way up) is interesting — suggests the errors stopped because the workspaces stopped working entirely

## Reflection
- **Sentiment:** Mixed signals — quotas are clean but unhealthy workspace count is concerning
- **Big-picture patterns:** Metrics can be misleading. Low error counts might mean the thing generating errors has died, not that things are healthy. Need to think about what "healthy" actually means.

## Ideas
- Redefine workspace health metrics: separate "alive but erroring" from "dead/unreachable" — these need different responses
- Correlate Sentry drops with workspace reachability — if errors drop because workspaces died, that's worse, not better
- Auto-terminate persistently unhealthy workspaces that aren't responding to health checks
- Health check that validates the full stack (agent running + gateway responding + SSH reachable) not just one signal
